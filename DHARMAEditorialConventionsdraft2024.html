[a]Shall we change the terminology (here and in the next EGD) from "intrinsic" to "logical" to match the names of the display alternatives?

[b]Was "verses", which is wrong. There were also two more similar rows with variations, which I consider unnecessary.

[c]Shall we change the terminology (here and in the next EGD) from "extrinsic" to "physical" to match the names of the display alternatives?

[d]I still think line numbers should be shown in ⟨⟩ (meaning “editorial addition” while () means “unclear”, which is inappropriate). I agree with keeping the grey colour and suggest, but do not insist, that superscript be used for these.

I suggest the same for the display of all pb and milestone elements.

[e]I propose discarding vowel and consonant from this, since we have no display for this markup, and anyway it's all half-baked and hardly anybody uses it.

[f]Depending on whether we discard those, it may be possible to give this section a better title; or to split it into two separate sections, for Symbols and Blank space.

[g]I wonder if we are making the right kinds of distinction in display. I have not checked these on dharman.in, so current display may be different. But from what we have in the table:

- we distinguish lost/illegible/undefined (using +/×/*) when exact or approximate number is encoded

- we do not make the same distinction when the extent is unknown.

This seems inconsistent. I think it would be best never to make the distinction in the display, only in the tooltip, so we would have only 3 rows here instead of 9, and 3 different displays instead of 7. The displays could be
